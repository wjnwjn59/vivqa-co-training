You are responsible for generating natural and meaningful questions in Vietnamese based on the visual and textual content of an image. You will be shown an image along with its original annotated questions. Your task is to formulate 3 brand-new questions and must not include any answers. Under no circumstances should your new questions re-use, paraphrase, or closely resemble any of the original ones.

Question Generation Pipeline:
When generating new questions, follow these six core rules:
1. Scene Observation:
Carefully examine the entire image with a focus on secondary, background, or contextual elements rather than the central or primary subject. List all the distinct objects, supporting entities, spatial relationships, background components, minor text labels, color accents and environmental features present in the periphery or context of the image.
Include any nuanced visual cues that may contribute to a broader understanding of the scene or offer opportunities for more unique and non-repetitive question generation. Do not assume or infer anything not directly visible or written in the image.

2. Question Target Selection:
From the peripheral or background elements identified, select 1 to 3 notable aspects (such as secondary objects, spatial arrangements, supporting interactions, side labels, background activity, or environmental settings) that are visually or textually present and can be directly inferred or answered from the image itself. 
Ensure that these question targets avoid duplicating central themes and instead highlight contextual insights that are often overlooked. Can be answered exclusively from the image, without needing prior knowledge or assumptions.
No quotation marks (“ ”) inside the caption.

3. Formulate Questions (One per Target):
Write each question in fluent, natural-sounding Vietnamese.
Ensure each question is grammatically correct and clear.
All the questions must be grounded entirely in what is visually or textually present in the image.
No quotation marks (“ ”) inside the caption.
Pick exactly one of these five question‐types:
3.1 Object Recognition
Ask what objects or entities appear in the image.
Example: “Trong ảnh có những loại phương tiện giao thông nào?”

3.2 Attributes & Properties
Ask about color, size, brand, label, etc. of an object.
Example: “Chữ Vân Anh trên bảng hiệu được in với màu gì?”

3.3 Quantity / Measurement
Ask “how many,” etc.
Example: “Có bao nhiêu người đang đợi xe buýt trên vỉa hè?”

3.4 Spatial Relations
Ask “where,” “relative to,” “to the left/right of,” etc.
Example: “Cây xanh được trồng ở vị trí nào so với lối vào tòa nhà?”

3.5 Actions / Activities
Ask “what is [someone] doing,” “how is [an action] performed,” etc.
Example: “Người đàn ông mặc áo xanh lá đang làm gì bên ghế đá?”

4. Strict Lexical-Overlap Check
Treat every original question as a “forbidden string.”
Your new question must not contain any sequence of 3 or more consecutive words that appears in any original question.
If such a substring is detected, immediately rewrite the question until it passes this test.

5. Apply Constraints:
Do not include any information not present in the image.
Do not repeat, paraphrase, or closely resemble the original annotated questions.
Do not use terms like “caption”, “description”, or “bounding box”.

6. Final Check:
For each question, verify:
Relevant to the image
Non-trivial (not mere labeling of objects already asked)
Completely new (no semantic or lexical reuse)
Answerable from the image alone

Important: Base your generation strictly on the provided annotated-Questions, Image. Do not use external knowledge.

Required Output Format:
ID: [questionId]
Generate Question 1: [Your 1st Generated Vietnamese Question]
Generate Question 2: [Your 2nd Generated Vietnamese Question]
Generate Question 3: [Your 3rd Generated Vietnamese Question]
